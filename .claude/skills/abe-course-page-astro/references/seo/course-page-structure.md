# Course Page Structure — hero stack + the ten content sections

**When to read:** Step 4 (writing) and Step 6 (pre-production) for any course page. Applies to Owner Builder, ASQA-accredited (White Card), and CPD pages.

**Provenance:** distilled from `COURSE-PAGE-CONTENT-TEMPLATE.md` v1.13 (17 June 2026), the working template behind the QLD Owner Builder reference build. Companions: `COURSE-PAGE-DESIGN-PRINCIPLES.md` (how it looks), `COURSE-PAGE-READABILITY.md` (format-time rules).

**Authority gate:** every section below is subject to `authority-model.md`. ABE is not an RTO. State-approved courses carry no "accredited" / "qualification" / "statement of attainment" language; the award is a Certificate of Completion. RTO-partner pages carry the ASQA disclosure per `asqa-disclosure-framework.md`.

## 1. Rule of the template

- Decision-critical content is **always fully visible** — never behind a tab or accordion.
- **One answer capsule per section**, as the lead (40–60 words).
- **Max one callout per section**, type-matched.
- **One verification block per section**, before the micro-CTA.
- **One money-CTA verb page-wide.**
- Never use "comprehensive". Australian English. No em dashes in body copy.

---

## 2. The arc

The page is an argument, not a list:

> **orient → qualify → regulator detail → explain → prove → price → widen → reassure → differentiate → vouch → convert**

Every section does one job in that arc and answers one question the reader is actually holding. The **same ten roles carry across any course page** (owner builder, white card, CPD, bundles) — only the subject changes.

---

## 3. Hero (above the fold)

Fixed element stack:

| Element | Spec |
|---|---|
| Eyebrow (approval pill) | `{{REGULATOR}}-Approved · {{COURSE_IDENTIFIER}}` |
| H1 | 50–60 chars, sentence case, carries the primary keyword; both keyword orders split across H1/meta |
| Subheadline | The requirement in one sentence, plus the action |
| Ticks | Desktop 4 / mobile 3 — friction-removers (approval, delivery, award, validity) |
| CTA | The one money-CTA verb, page-wide, with the price in it |
| CTA microcopy | Instant access · award on completion · payment options |
| Price | Shown in full. **No click-to-reveal.** |
| Trust row | Max 3 items |
| Visual + alt | Certificate artefact; alt 80–125 chars per `alt-text-guidelines.md` |
| How-it-works line | Enrol → Complete → Get certificate → Apply to the regulator |

**Never in the hero:** security marks, money-back guarantees.

---

## 4. Overview / data strip

A single wayfinding layer. Five decision-critical fact cells, figure-first — e.g. permit trigger, course fee, government fee, duration, eligibility. Decision-critical only; not a dumping ground.

---

## 5. The ten sections

### §1 · What is the course — orient
- **Job:** establish what the course is and why it exists, before any detail. (Whether they personally *need* it is §2.)
- **Reader's question:** "What is this?"
- **Carries:** answer capsule (the definition) → the **quick-facts summary table** → short body → see-also linking **UP to the hub** (per internal-linking rules) → micro-CTA to §2.

**Quick-facts summary table** — nine operational rows, fixed order: Course · Duration · Delivery · Assessment · Award (+ identifier) · When you receive it · How you receive it · Regulator · Expiry.

Keep to these nine **operational** facts. Eligibility/prerequisites (§2) and price (§6) own their own sections — do not duplicate them here. The table repeats facts as data, not new copy.

### §2 · Do I need it? — qualify (the gate)
- **Job:** resolve whether the reader needs the course and the permit/role it leads to, and what that permit lets them do. **The single home for all eligibility and permit detail — there is no second permit section.**
- **Reader's question:** "Is this me? Do I qualify, and what does it let me do?"
- **Carries:** answer capsule (the yes/no + trigger/threshold) → eligibility (keep **enrol prerequisites** distinct from **role/permit eligibility**) → a **can / can't panel** → at most one type-matched callout for the sharp caveat → a sourced fact link.
- Decision-critical: never behind a tab.

### §3 · From the {{REGULATOR}} — regulator detail / authority (E-E-A-T + SEO depth)
- **Job:** the authoritative permit detail that doesn't fit the §2 decision — responsibilities, thresholds, exemptions, **how to apply for the permit**, other key rules — drawn from the regulator's own resources.
- **Reader's question:** "What does the regulator actually require of me?"
- **Role:** deepens authority right after the permit decision, and is the page's home for the **informational long-tail surfaced by the competitor-gap / keyword analysis**.
- **Carries:** eyebrow `From the {{REGULATOR}}` → short intro → **2–4 labelled sub-blocks**, each a digest of one regulator topic → one verification block citing the regulator page each fact came from.
- **The "how to apply for the permit" sub-block** hands the reader to the next step: the steps, what to lodge (**including this course's certificate**), where/how to lodge, and the application fee. Short numbered list, sourced and cited. Distinct from §4 (how the *course* works).
- **Boundary:** reference depth, not the decision. The headline "do I need a permit + threshold" answer stays in §2.

### §4 · How it works — explain the mechanism
- **Job:** remove process friction. Show the path from enrol to outcome is short and low-commitment.
- **Carries:** a short stepper (enrol → complete → receive + act on the award), each step one-line heading + 1–2 sentences. No more than the real number of steps.
- **Assessment lives here**, inside the *complete* step. Source it from the course materials and mark `[TO VERIFY]` until confirmed — **never assume format, question count, pass mark, or retries.**

### §5 · What you'll learn — prove capability
- **Job:** show the course is substantive, via both **scope** and **outcomes**.
- **Carries two distinct blocks:**
  - **Topics (scope)** — scannable grid of the course's actual modules, **taken from the curriculum, never invented**.
  - **Training outcomes (capability)** — checked list of what the learner can do on completion, from the course's **stated learning outcomes**. Phrase as capabilities.
- **Source rule:** both from course materials. `[TO VERIFY]` until confirmed. **Do not pad with generic items.**

### §6 · Pricing — price honestly
- **Job:** state the full cost with nothing hidden, separating what ABE charges from any third-party/government fee.
- **Carries:** price panel — provider fee + separate external fee = clearly marked **total** → note on payment options and which fees are outside ABE's control.

### §7 · What else your build needs — related requirements & next steps
- **Job:** point to the *other* regulator requirements the reader's build triggers (additional required training, insurance) and route them to ABE's courses. **Cross-promotion framed as compliance help, not upsell.**
- **Carries two sub-blocks:** other required/recommended training; insurance.
- **Discipline:** distinguish **required from recommended** — never call something mandatory unless the regulator says so. Cross-promote **up / related only, never sideways to other states** (no-cannibalisation). **Never imply ABE supplies insurance it doesn't** — inform and link to the authoritative source.
- **Length cap:** each sub-block is a pointer, not an explainer — **≤ 3 items, ≤ 60 words total**. Overflow is the signal to spin out a spoke page and link to it.

### §8 · FAQ — reassure / mop up
- **Job:** answer residual questions and feed `FAQPage` schema.
- **Carries:** accordion, 7–8 Q/As, each answer 30–80 words, **no CTAs inside answers**. Mirror every Q/A verbatim into schema. Include an assessment question whose answer matches the verified §4 / §1-table wording.

### §9 · Why ABE — differentiate
- **Job:** why this provider, in **verifiable terms** (track record, approval, support), not adjectives.
- **Carries:** short numbered list of concrete, checkable claims. No "comprehensive", no puffery.

### §10 · Content review / expert — vouch (E-E-A-T)
- **Job:** attach a named, credentialed human with a review date, so authority is verifiable.
- **Carries:** review-process line (developed by / reviewed by + date) and expert card(s) — name, role, credentials pulled **live from the Notion Experts DB, never hardcoded**; profile link. Any quote stays flagged as placeholder until the expert approves. Fallback: `references/expert-fallback/`.

## 6. Unnumbered moments

Data strip, trust band, and closing CTA **punctuate** the arc rather than advance the argument, so they are not numbered sections. Closing CTA band repeats the one money-CTA verb. A consolidated **Sources** section closes the page (named, dated links); every verification block points to it.

---

## 7. Sourcing rules (non-negotiable)

- Every government/regulatory fact is **summarised from the official source and cited**. Visible citation is a **publish hard blocker** (`quality-gates.md` §4).
- **Summarise and rephrase; never copy regulator text verbatim.**
- Mark `[TO VERIFY]` until traced to a source. Unresolved government facts block publish.
- Course facts ABE controls (price, assessment, modules, reviewer date) that are unconfirmed are marked `[confirm: LW]` — never guessed.
- Choose §3 sub-block topics from (a) what the regulator resources actually contain, and (b) the **competitor-gap / keyword analysis** — cover the intents competitors rank for that ABE doesn't yet.

## 8. Authority-gated variations by course type

| Course type | Authority position | Section deltas |
|---|---|---|
| **Owner Builder** (state-approved, ABE-direct) | ABE delivers under the state regulator's approval. **No** ASQA/RTO/accredited language; award is a Certificate of Completion. | Full ten sections as above. §3 is the regulator section. |
| **White Card** (ASQA-accredited, RTO partner) | Delivered by the RTO partner. ASQA disclosure required in the 7 per-course-page locations. Award is a Statement of Attainment **issued by the RTO**, not ABE. | §3 becomes the WHS regulator/scheme section. Delivery mode is state-gated — see `online-delivery-policy-by-state.md`; **never** claim self-paced online outside WA/TAS. |
| **CPD** | Points/hours against a licence; state-regulated. | §2 becomes "do these points count for my licence"; §5 carries the points breakdown. See `cbos-tas-reference.md` for TAS. |
| **Bundles** | Inherits the authority position of each included course. | §5 lists the included courses; §7 is usually redundant — fold into §5. |
