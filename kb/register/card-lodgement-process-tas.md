# ABE Education — White Card Lodgement Process (Tasmania)

How a Tasmanian White Card applicant turns their Statement of Attainment into a physical construction induction card through Service Tasmania. Use for the TAS White Card course-page "after the course / how to get your card" section, the FAQ, and post-purchase guidance.

**Verified:** 27 May 2026 against service.tas.gov.au and worksafe.tas.gov.au. **Re-verify:** quarterly, and before publishing any dollar figure or timeframe in body copy. Tasmania is the **only** ABE White Card jurisdiction where the applicant lodges in person and pays a separate government fee — do not generalise this process to QLD, WA or ACT (those issue differently; see `state-fees-register.md`).

ABE's White Card is delivered by RTO partner **Blue Dog Training (RTO 31193)**; ABE is the enrolment partner. The physical card is issued by **WorkSafe Tasmania** through **Service Tasmania** — not by ABE or the RTO. (Regulator split: WorkSafe Tasmania handles WHS and the White Card; CBOS handles owner builder and CPD. Never attribute the White Card to CBOS.)

---

## 1. Process at a glance

1. Complete CPCWHS1001 (*Prepare to work safely in the construction industry*) with a Tasmanian RTO, in Tasmania → receive a Statement of Attainment (SOA).
2. Within **60 days** of completing the training, lodge a construction induction card application **in person at a Service Tasmania service centre**.
3. Pay the **$13.37** application fee.
4. Service Tasmania reviews the documents and forwards them to WorkSafe Tasmania for processing.
5. WorkSafe Tasmania posts the physical card. If it has not arrived within 60 days of lodgement, the applicant contacts WorkSafe Tasmania.

---

## 2. The 60-day rule

- The application must be lodged within **60 days** of the SOA issue date.
- If lodging **after** 60 days, the applicant must also provide a **written declaration from the RTO** confirming they successfully completed the training.
- Where the RTO is the Department for Education, Children and Young People (DECYP) and an SOA is not provided, a signed declaration letter from the DECYP Director/CEO is required instead.

---

## 3. Documents required at lodgement

- a completed **Construction Induction Card Application** form (the official WorkSafe Tasmania form)
- the **SOA**, which must show the applicant's name, the RTO number, the date of issue, and the unit code **CPCWHS1001** — an SOA missing any of these is rejected
- **evidence of identity** (see §4)
- the **fee** (see §5)

---

## 4. Proving identity

The applicant must provide original, current documents (not photocopies or certified copies):

- **two** documents from Category A, **or**
- **one** Category A document **plus two** Category B documents.

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| **A** | Australian birth certificate (not an extract), citizenship papers, Australian passport (accepted if expired up to 2 years), Australian driver licence, Australian firearm licence, Tasmanian Government personal ID card, Home Affairs travel document / ImmiCard, Certificate of Evidence of Resident Status, overseas passport |
| **B** | Medicare card, plastic bank/credit card with signature, DVA or Centrelink pensioner concession card, BDM change-of-name or marriage certificate, security/crowd-controller licence, government photo ID card, WorkSafe Tasmania high-risk-work or other photo licence, student ID card, visa grant/VEVO (with an overseas passport) |

A change of name requires the standard A/B combination **plus** linking proof (original BDM marriage or change-of-name certificate). Applicants aged 16 or under can use a birth certificate, passport, a signed school attendance statement on letterhead, or a student ID card.

---

## 5. Fees

| Transaction | Government fee |
|---|---|
| New construction induction card application | **$13.37** |
| Replacement card | **$13.37** |

Fees and charges last updated **1 July 2025** (Service Tasmania). Payment at the service centre by cash, cheque, EFTPOS, Visa or Mastercard. This fee is separate from, and additional to, the course fee the student pays ABE.

---

## 6. Replacement cards

- Lodge a **Construction Induction Replacement Application** form at a Service Tasmania centre, with the original card number (if known) and proof of identity, and pay the $13.37 replacement fee.
- The original card must have been **issued in Tasmania**. A card issued in another state must be replaced through that state's regulator.

---

## 7. Eligibility and recognition

- Minimum age to apply for the card: **14**.
- A person who already holds a **current, valid interstate** general construction induction card does **not** need a Tasmanian card — interstate cards are recognised nationally.
- The card does not carry an expiry date, but it is voided if the holder is out of the construction industry for **2 consecutive years**, after which the training must be redone.

---

## 8. How to present this on the page

- Make the **two-cost structure** explicit: the course fee paid to ABE, and the separate **$13.37** government fee paid at Service Tasmania. Students otherwise assume the course price is the whole cost.
- Do **not** state that ABE or Blue Dog Training issues the card — the card is issued by WorkSafe Tasmania via Service Tasmania once the SOA is lodged.
- Surface the **60-day window** prominently; it is the most common point of failure.
- Note that lodgement is **in person** at a Service Tasmania centre (not online) — this is specific to Tasmania.
- Refer to the unit as **CPCWHS1001** and the card as the **construction induction card** / **White Card**. Australian English throughout.
