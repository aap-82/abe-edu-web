# CBOS Tasmania Reference — CPD + Owner Builder

**When to read:** any TAS page — CPD (points-by-licence, approval rule) or Owner Builder (permit trigger, course requirement). Feeds Step 1 fact-spine and Step 4 content.

**Regulator:** Consumer, Building and Occupational Services (CBOS), a division of the Tasmanian Department of Justice. Contact: cbos.info@justice.tas.gov.au. CBOS licenses building service providers, electrical workers, plumbers, gas-fitters and automotive gas-fitters, and issues owner builder permits.

**Authority position (per `authority-model.md`):** ABE is NOT an RTO. ABE's TAS CPD and Owner Builder courses are **CBOS-approved**, delivered by ABE directly (owner builder) or co-developed with an RTO partner where the unit is nationally accredited (e.g. White Card CPCWHS1001, asbestos awareness). Award for the owner builder course is a Certificate of Completion, not a qualification or Statement of Attainment. Use "CBOS-approved", never "accredited", for the owner builder course.

**Fact-sourcing:** every figure below is a `.gov.au`-verified primary fact. Re-verify on cadence — CBOS fees reset **1 July** annually, and the points Determinations are periodically amended. ## Part A — CPD

### A1. Points-by-licence table (minimum annual CPD points)

Source: CBOS "Achieving your CPD requirements" + Occupational Licensing (Building Services Work) Determination and Occupational Licensing (Continuing Professional Development) Determination.

| Licence category | Minimum CPD points / year |
|---|---|
| Builder | 12 |
| Building Designer | 20 |
| Architect | 20 |
| Civil Designer | 20 |
| Designer Specialising in Building Services (Building Services Designer) | 20 |
| Building Surveyor | 30 |
| Engineer | 30 |
| Plumbers / Gas-fitters (and endorsement holders) | per-module allocation — `[VERIFY AT BUILD]` against the current Determination for the specific class/endorsement |

The CPD year runs against the licence renewal cycle; the licence holder confirms CPD compliance at renewal. Point allocations for specific courses are set by the Administrator of Occupational Licensing.

### A2. Multi-licence cap (important, frequently mis-stated)

A person holding **several** licence categories does **not** add the requirements together. They meet the **highest single** requirement, not the sum. Worked example (from CBOS): someone licensed as both a **builder (12)** and a **building designer (20)** needs **20 points total per year, not 32** — though at least some of those points should be relevant to the builder work. State this cap on any CPD page that could be read by multi-licence holders.

### A3. Category caps within the total

- **WHS courses:** CBOS allows a **maximum of 4 WHS points per renewal year**. Do not imply a practitioner can fill their whole requirement with WHS content.
- Persons holding licences in several categories have a cap on combined points (see A2).
- `[VERIFY AT BUILD]` any other per-category cap relevant to the specific course being written.

### A4. Course approval rule (the CBOS gate)

- CPD points are only recognised where the course is **approved by the Administrator of Occupational Licensing**. "Approved" is course-specific — a provider being listed somewhere does not make every one of its courses approved (CBOS states this explicitly about Pointsbuild: only the specifically listed courses count).
- CBOS requires **all proposed CPD training/activities to be submitted for approval and/or notification** — including internal training and toolbox meetings.
- On a page: claim CBOS approval only for courses ABE has actual approval for, and mark the specific point value `[confirm: LW]` until verified against ABE's records. Never assert a point value from memory.

### A5. Record-keeping (useful FAQ content)

Responsibility for recording CPD sits with the **licence holder** (employees keep their own record and ensure the employer's register reflects it; sole practitioners are wholly responsible). Records should be retained and portable between employers. Good source for a "how do I track my CPD" FAQ.

## Part B — Owner Builder

### B1. Permit trigger + threshold

- An Owner Builder Permit is issued only for **residential** building work (Class 1a detached dwelling or conjoined unit; plus Class 10 structures and, in defined cases, Class 8 farm sheds per s.29A(1) of the Act). **No permit for commercial buildings.**
- **Permit threshold: `[VERIFY AT BUILD]`.** Sources conflict — a third-party guide cites A$5,000 while other material references the A$20,000 residential-contract line under the Residential Building Work Contracts and Dispute Resolution Act 2016. These are two different thresholds (permit trigger vs contract-regulation trigger) and must be separated and confirmed against the current CBOS owner-builder pages before publish. Do not state a single dollar figure without live verification.
- Some low-risk work needs no permit (e.g. shed/garage/carport up to 18m², or 36m² if prefabricated; porch/veranda up to 9m²; deck up to 1m). A building permit from council may still be required for notifiable/permit work even where an owner builder permit is not.

### B2. Course requirement (the ABE hook)

- To apply for an owner builder permit for a residence, the applicant must have **completed an approved Tasmanian owner builder course within the last 12 months**, **and** a **White Card (Work Safely in the Construction Industry, CPCWHS1001)**.
- **Only Tasmanian-approved courses are accepted** — CBOS is explicit that interstate owner builder courses do not satisfy this. This is ABE's core TAS owner builder selling point: a CBOS-approved TAS-specific course.
- Both are delivered fully online; certificates issued on completion.

### B3. Frequency + eligibility restrictions

- **Only two owner builder Class 1a projects per person in any ten-year period** (the two may be two entirely new dwellings). This is the **strictest frequency limit in Australia** — worth stating plainly.
- Each new project needs a **new** permit; owner builders cannot keep doing further work on what they have already built under an old permit.
- Applicant must be a **natural person** owning the land — **a company cannot hold a permit**. A person who has signed a contract to buy the land/building also counts as an "owner".

### B4. Insurance + resale

- **Insurance:** owner builders must hold public and construction liability insurance of **not less than A$5 million**. Fee and insurance requirements are the **same as a licensed builder**. `[VERIFY AT BUILD]` the exact figure and wording against the current CBOS fact sheet.
- **No mandatory home warranty insurance scheme exists in Tasmania** for building work (including owner builder). Do not imply ABE or any affiliate provides a home-warranty product; ABE may reference an affiliate broker for the required liability cover, framed as information, not a guarantee (`authority-model.md` insurance rule).
- **Resale disclosure** of owner builder status is required on the contract for sale.

### B5. Application path (for the §3 "how to apply" sub-block)

1. Develop plans with a licensed Building Designer or Architect; engage a Building Surveyor to certify.
2. Complete the **approved TAS owner builder course** (within 12 months) and the **White Card**.
3. Put the required **liability insurance** in place before applying.
4. Complete the CBOS **Application for owner builder permit** form; attach supporting documents.
5. Submit at any **Service Tasmania** shop (or email/post to CBOS) and pay the fee (`[VERIFY AT BUILD]` — resets 1 July).
6. On success, CBOS issues a confirmation letter with a permit number; then apply for a **building permit at your local council**.
