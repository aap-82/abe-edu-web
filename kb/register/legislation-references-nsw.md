# Legislation References — New South Wales

Key NSW legislation for ABE's NSW owner builder and (where referenced) White Card content. Use for course-page legal references and compliance statements.

**Verified:** 26 May 2026 against legislation.nsw.gov.au, nsw.gov.au (Building Commission NSW) and SafeWork NSW. **Re-verify:** annually — NSW residential building law is under active reform (see the Building Bill 2024 flag). **Confirm section numbers against the current consolidated Act before quoting.**

---

## 1. Building and owner builder

- ***Home Building Act 1989* (NSW)** — the core residential building law: licensing of residential building work, **owner-builder permits**, statutory warranties (major / non-major defects), and the Home Building Compensation scheme. Administered by **Building Commission NSW**, which took over NSW Fair Trading's home-building functions (Building Commission NSW established 1 December 2023). The insurance "Authority" under the Act is the **State Insurance Regulatory Authority (SIRA)**.
  - ⚠️ **Pending reform:** the **Building Bill 2024 / Building Act 2024 (NSW)** is expected to **replace the *Home Building Act 1989***, with changes to contracts and warranties. Monitor before publishing long-life NSW content.
- ***Environmental Planning and Assessment Act 1979* (NSW)** — development consent and complying development. An owner-builder permit is required where the work both exceeds the value threshold **and** needs development consent or a complying development certificate under this Act.
- ***Design and Building Practitioners Act 2020* (NSW)** — registration and duties of designers and builders for regulated residential building work (broader reform context).
- **Owner-builder specifics:** permit required for residential work over **$10,000** (incl. labour + materials) needing consent/CDC; an **approved course** (the required units of competency, or approved equivalent) is mandatory where work is valued over **$20,000**; **one permit per 5 years** (exceptions: same land, or special circumstances via Form 7); must reside as principal place of residence; resale disclosure obligations run **7.5 years**. Applications are made via **Service NSW**.

## 2. WHS / White Card

- ***Work Health and Safety Act 2011* (NSW)** + ***Work Health and Safety Regulation 2017* (NSW)** — an owner-builder is a **person conducting a business or undertaking (PCBU)** for WHS purposes (confirmed in *Visscher v SafeWork NSW* [2024]). Regulator: **SafeWork NSW**.
- **Construction induction ("White Card"):** the unit **CPCWHS1001** *Prepare to work safely in the construction industry*. In NSW, SafeWork NSW issues a **Statement of Training** (valid 60 days) pending issue of the card. White Card training must be completed **in person** in NSW (online delivery is restricted to WA and TAS residents).
