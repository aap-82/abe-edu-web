# Legislation References — Western Australia

Key WA legislation for ABE's WA owner builder and (where referenced) White Card content. Use for course-page legal references and compliance statements.

**Verified:** 26 May 2026 against legislation.wa.gov.au and wa.gov.au (Building and Energy / WorkSafe WA). **Re-verify:** annually. **Confirm section numbers against the current consolidated Act before quoting one in body copy** — section numbers are the detail most likely to drift.

---

## 1. Building and owner builder

- ***Building Act 2011* (WA)** — building permits and the framework for "builder work". A registered building contractor is required for builder work valued over **$20,000**; below that (or for excluded work) no registered contractor is required.
- ***Building Services (Registration) Act 2011* (WA)** — registration of building practitioners and contractors, **and** owner-builder approval. Owner-builder approval is granted by the **Building Services Board**. Section **45(2)** allows the Board to waive the prohibition on granting approval to someone issued an owner-builder building permit in the preceding **6 years**. Section **99** sets penalties up to **$25,000** for false or misleading information in an application.
- ***Building Services (Complaint Resolution and Administration) Act 2011* (WA)** — building-services complaints.
- **Owner-builder specifics:** apply via **Form 75** (waiver via **Form 76** / s45(2)); approval required where building work exceeds **$20,000**; the dwelling generally **cannot be sold within 7 years** of the owner-builder building permit being issued unless prescribed conditions are met.
- **Regulator:** Building Services Board, supported by the **Building and Energy** division within the **Department of Local Government, Industry Regulation and Safety (LGIRS)**. (LGIRS was formerly DEMIRS — the Department of Energy, Mines, Industry Regulation and Safety — renamed 1 July 2025; official domain now lgirs.wa.gov.au.)

## 2. WHS / White Card

- ***Work Health and Safety Act 2020* (WA)** — assented 10 November 2020; **operational 31 March 2022**; based on the national model WHS laws. Includes industrial manslaughter and Category 1/2/3 offences.
- ***Work Health and Safety (General) Regulations 2022* (WA)** — the general-workplace regulations relevant to construction. (Separate Mines and Petroleum WHS regulations also exist.)
- **Regulator:** WorkSafe WA (the WorkSafe Commissioner), within LGIRS.
- **Construction induction ("White Card"):** the nationally recognised unit *Prepare to work safely in the construction industry* (**CPCWHS1001** — verified against training.gov.au, 26 May 2026; supersedes and is equivalent to the earlier CPCCWHS1001). Both the 'white' card and the WA 'blue' card are accepted as evidence of induction.
