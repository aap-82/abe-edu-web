# ABE Education — WHS Penalties by State

Work health and safety penalty information for ABE's construction-safety content (White Card pages especially): what a worker or PCBU faces for breaching a WHS duty, and the much smaller, more directly relevant offence of carrying out construction work without a construction induction (White Card) card. Use for "why the White Card matters / what happens without one" sections and FAQs.

**Verified:** 27 May 2026 against Safe Work Australia and SafeWork NSW. **Re-verify:** annually, immediately after 1 July — WHS maximum penalties and penalty-unit values are indexed to CPI each financial year. Always confirm a specific maximum against the current source before publishing it as a figure; these maxima are large, differ by state, and are amended often. Treat every dollar figure here as current-as-at the date shown, not permanent.

**Scope.** WHS penalties for ABE's White Card states — **NSW, QLD, WA, TAS, ACT** (NSW added 2026-05-28 via the Upskill Institute partnership; its figures are also the most fully documented and serve as the worked example). Owner-builder "no permit" penalties sit under **building** law, not the WHS Act, and are out of scope — see the note in §4.

**The one source to trust for current figures:** Safe Work Australia's *Maximum monetary penalties under the WHS laws* page and its cross-jurisdictional table (both in §5). They are recalculated each 1 July and are the canonical reference for the current maximum in each jurisdiction. Where this file gives a state figure, it is illustrative — confirm against that table before publishing.

## 1. How WHS penalties are expressed (it differs by state)

There is no single national WHS penalty figure. The mechanism differs:

- **NSW** expresses its WHS penalties in **penalty units** (a dollar value × the number of units set for the offence), under WHS Act s242B. This is why NSW maxima are cleanly computable (see §2).
- The **model WHS Act** and **ACT** express WHS maxima as **indexed dollar amounts**, recalculated each 1 July.
- **TAS** adopted the model WHS Act; its WHS maxima track the model dollar amounts.
- **WA** has its **own dollar penalty scale** under the *Work Health and Safety Act 2020* (WA), and its Category 1 offence is defined differently — it requires conduct that **caused** death or serious harm, rather than the model's reckless/grossly-negligent **exposure** to risk.
- **QLD** adopted the model WHS Act with its own (generally higher) dollar maxima; the QLD general penalty-unit value below is provided for context only.

### Penalty-unit values (2025–26) — context

| Jurisdiction | Penalty unit (2025–26) | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **NSW** | **$123.31** | WHS Act s242B; indexed each 1 July (was $120.42 in 2024–25). Directly drives the NSW WHS maxima in §2. |
| **QLD** | **$161.30** | Penalties and Sentences Act value; rising to **$172.70 from 1 July 2026**. General context — confirm QLD WHS maxima on the SWA table. |
| **TAS** | ⚠️ gazetted before 1 July each year — confirm | TAS WHS maxima track the model dollar amounts; read off the SWA table. |

## 2. WHS duty-of-care offences (the large penalties)

The model WHS Act sets three offence categories for breaching a health-and-safety duty (such as the PCBU's primary duty under s19), plus an industrial manslaughter offence:

- **Category 1** — reckless or grossly negligent conduct exposing a person to a risk of death or serious injury (in WA: conduct that **caused** death or serious harm). The most serious; includes imprisonment.
- **Category 2** — failure to comply with a duty that exposes a person to a risk of death or serious injury.
- **Category 3** — failure to comply with a duty (no exposure element).
- **Industrial manslaughter** — now enacted in every state. Model maximum: **$20,441,000** for a body corporate and **20 years' imprisonment** for an individual (as at 1 July 2025).

### NSW worked figures (2025–26, at $123.31 per unit) — illustrative

| Offence (NSW WHS Act) | Individual | Individual as PCBU / officer | Body corporate |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Category 1** (s31) | $1,114,475 (up to 10 years' prison) | $2,318,844 (up to 10 years' prison) | $11,150,183 |
| **Category 2** (s32) | $223,561 | $447,122 | confirm on the SafeWork NSW schedule |

Other states' figures differ — read them off the Safe Work Australia cross-jurisdictional table (§5). Do not assume NSW figures apply in QLD, WA, TAS or ACT.

## 3. The White Card offence (most relevant to White Card pages)

This is the offence ABE's White Card pages should reference. It is **separate from, and far smaller than, the Category 1–3 duty offences above.**

Under the WHS Regulations (model regs in the r.317–r.319 range):

- a **PCBU must not direct or allow a worker** to carry out construction work unless the worker has completed general construction induction training (holds a White Card); and
- the **worker must hold the card and produce it** to the PCBU or an inspector on request.

These are **regulation-level** offences, carrying modest penalty amounts well below the Act's duty-offence tiers. ⚠️ The exact amount varies by each jurisdiction's WHS Regulations — confirm the current figure against the relevant state regulations before publishing a dollar amount.

**The escalation point worth making on-page:** if an uninducted worker is involved in an incident, the PCBU's failure can be prosecuted under the **primary-duty (Category 1–3) offences** in §2 — that is the source of the six- and seven-figure penalties. The White Card requirement is the cheap, mandatory front line; ignoring it does not cap the exposure at the small induction-offence fine.

## 4. How to present this on the page

- Lead with the **legal requirement** — a White Card is mandatory before doing construction work — not with a scare number.
- If citing a figure, use the **PCBU/officer or body-corporate Category penalties** for "what an employer faces", and be explicit that it is a court-set **maximum**, not an automatic fine, and that amounts are indexed and differ by state.
- Never quote a single national penalty figure as if it applied everywhere — WHS maxima differ by jurisdiction and by who the duty-holder is (worker vs PCBU vs body corporate).
- Australian English; use **"WHS"**, never "OH&S"; treat the worker duty and the PCBU duty as distinct.

**Out of scope (note for a future build):** penalties for owner-builder work carried out **without a permit** are set under building law — the *Home Building Act 1989* (NSW), *QBCC Act 1991* (QLD), *Building Services (Registration) Act 2011* (WA), *Occupational Licensing Act 2005* (TAS) and *Construction Occupations (Licensing) Act 2004* (ACT) — not the WHS Act. They belong in a future `penalties-owner-builder-by-state.md` or the relevant `legislation-references-[state].md`, not here.
