# PPE Requirements (AS/NZS Standards)

The personal protective equipment standards relevant to ABE's White Card and construction-safety course content. Use for course-page PPE sections and assessment content.

**Verified:** 26 May 2026 against multiple Australian WHS/PPE references. **Re-verify:** annually against the Standards Australia catalogue — **standard numbers stay stable but editions/years change**, so confirm the current edition before citing a year in body copy.

---

## 1. WHS context (state this first on any PPE page)

PPE is the **lowest tier in the hierarchy of controls** — it protects the individual but does nothing to remove the hazard, so it is used only where a risk cannot first be eliminated or minimised by higher-order controls. Under the model WHS Regulations, where PPE is the chosen control the person conducting the business or undertaking (PCBU) must **provide it at no cost** to workers, ensure it is suitable, a correct fit, clean, maintained, and that workers are trained in its use. This framing matters for White Card content: PPE is presented as a last line of defence, not the first.

## 2. Construction PPE standards

| PPE item | AS/NZS standard | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Head — hard hat / helmet** | AS/NZS 1801 (manufacture); AS/NZS 1800 (selection, care & use) | Required on most sites; secure with a chinstrap when working at heights or in wind. Metal helmets are not permitted around electrical risk. |
| **Hi-vis clothing** | AS/NZS 4602.1:2011; retroreflective material AS/NZS 1906.4:2010 | Garment classes: D (day), N (night), D/N (day + night). Mandatory for site workers around vehicle/plant movement. |
| **Footwear** | AS/NZS 2210.3 (safety footwear); AS/NZS 2210.1 (general/occupational) | Steel-cap or composite toe; protects against crush, puncture, chemicals. No sandals/thongs. |
| **Eye & face** | AS/NZS 1337.1 (eye protectors); AS/NZS 1338 (filters for welding etc.) | Selected by risk — impact, dust, chemical splash, radiation/welding. |
| **Hearing** | AS/NZS 1270 (hearing protectors); AS/NZS 1269.3 (selection/use) | Required where noise exceeds the exposure standard (85 dB(A) over 8 hours / 140 dB(C) peak). Audiometric testing obligations apply. |
| **Respiratory** | AS/NZS 1716 (respiratory protective devices); AS/NZS 1715 (selection, use & maintenance) | P1/P2/P3 filter classes; central to silica and hazardous-dust work. Fit testing applies. |
| **Fall protection — harness/lanyard** | AS/NZS 1891 series (AS/NZS 1891.4 selection, use & maintenance) | For work at heights (commonly referenced from 2 m); equipment must be certified and inspected. |
| **Sun protection (clothing)** | AS/NZS 4399 (UPF sun-protective clothing) | Outdoor work; complements (does not replace) shade/scheduling controls. |
| **Hands — gloves** | AS/NZS 2161 series (occupational protective gloves) — ⚠️ confirm current part | Task-selected: cut, chemical, heat or electrical-insulating; not always suitable for fine work. |

**Minimum site-entry PPE** is commonly: hard hat, hi-vis, safety footwear, eye protection and gloves — with hearing, respiratory and fall protection added by task and risk assessment.
