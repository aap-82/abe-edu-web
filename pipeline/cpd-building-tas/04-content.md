## H2 · Does twelve points cover your whole year?

**Answer capsule (49 words)**
> Twelve points meets a builder's requirement in full. If you also hold a building designer or
> architect licence you need 20, not 32: CBOS counts the highest single requirement you hold, never
> the sum. A building surveyor or engineer needs 30, so this bundle covers part of that year.

**Body**
The rule on multiple licences is the one most often got wrong, and it works in your favour. A person
licensed as both a builder and a building designer owes 20 points for the year, not the 32 they would
owe if the requirements stacked. Some of that learning should still relate to each type of work you
are licensed for.

If you hold only a builder licence, twelve points is your year, and this bundle is built to be
exactly that and no more. If you hold a surveyor or engineer licence at 30 points, buy it knowing it
covers twelve of them.

| Licence | Points a year |
|---|---|
| Builder | 12 |
| Building designer, architect, civil designer, building services designer | 20 |
| Building surveyor, engineer | 30 |

## H2 · How many of these points will actually count?

**Answer capsule (52 words)**
> CBOS counts at most four work health and safety points in a renewal year, whatever you complete
> beyond that. So the mix in a bundle matters as much as the total, because the points you are
> audited on are the ones that fall inside the caps, not the ones on your receipt.

**Body**
This is the part most bundles do not mention. A twelve-point bundle made mostly of safety courses
does not give a practitioner twelve countable points, because the WHS category is capped at four a
year. The total on the invoice and the total CBOS accepts are different numbers.

ABE mixes safety with trade and technical topics for this reason, rather than filling a bundle with
whichever courses were cheapest to build. What you should do before buying, if your renewal is close,
is check what you have already recorded this year. If you have used your safety points elsewhere, the
useful part of any bundle is the technical content, and that is worth knowing before you pay rather
than after.

## H2 · How does it work?

**Answer capsule (45 words)**
> Enrol once, work through the twelve courses in any order at your own pace, complete the assessment
> on each, and download a certificate as you finish each one. Keep those certificates with your own
> CPD record, because in Tasmania the record is yours to hold.

**Body**
Recording CPD sits with the licence holder, not the provider and not the employer. If you are
employed, keep your own copies and make sure your employer's register reflects them. If you are a
sole practitioner, the whole record is yours. Certificates are portable between employers, which
matters if you change jobs mid-cycle.

**Steps**
1. **Get the bundle.** One purchase covers all twelve courses.
2. **Work through them in any order.** Online, self-paced, on any device.
3. **Complete the assessment on each course.** Each one is assessed, not just read.
4. **Download your certificate as you go.** One per course, issued on completion.
5. **File them with your CPD record.** You confirm your CPD to CBOS when you renew.

## H2 · Common questions

**How do I record my CPD in Tasmania?**
You keep the record yourself. CBOS puts the responsibility on the licence holder: employees keep
their own record and make sure the employer's register matches it, and sole practitioners are
responsible for all of it. Keep your certificates, and confirm your CPD when you renew your licence.

**Are these courses actually approved by CBOS?**
Yes, and each one separately. CBOS approves individual courses rather than providers, so the fact
that a provider appears on a list does not mean everything it sells counts. Each course in this
bundle carries its own approval, and the table above shows the date each one runs to.

**I hold two licences. Do I need double the points?**
No. You meet the highest single requirement across the licences you hold, not the sum. A licensed
builder and building designer needs 20 points, not 32, although some of that learning should relate
to each type of work.

**Does this bundle cover a building surveyor or engineer?**
Partly. Surveyors and engineers need 30 points a year, so twelve of them come from this bundle and
the rest has to come from elsewhere. The courses themselves apply to those licences; the total does
not.

**What happens when a course approval expires?**
CBOS approvals run for two years. When one lapses, the course comes off this page and out of the
bundle, and the point count on this page changes with it. You keep any points you have already
completed and recorded.
