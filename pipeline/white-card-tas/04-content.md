## S1 · Is this a real White Card? {#real}

**Answer capsule**

**Body:**
The White Card market has known bad actors, so check the provider before you pay, not after. Blue Dog
Training holds CPCWHS1001 on its scope of registration and is approved to deliver and assess it in

31193. We would rather you did.

That is the difference between a real White Card and a worthless certificate: the unit code and the
RTO number, both verifiable, before any money changes hands.

## S2 · Is it accepted, and can I do it online in Tasmania? {#accepted}

**Answer capsule**
> Yes on both counts, with one condition. A White Card is nationally recognised the moment it is
> issued, accepted on every construction site in Australia. And because you live in Tasmania, you may
> complete the training fully online at your own pace. That online option is open to Tasmanian
> residents only.

**Body:**
Most sites will not let you start work without a White Card, and the doubt people arrive with is
whether an online one is treated any differently. It is not. Once issued, the card is the same
credential however the training was delivered.

The delivery rules are set by where you live, not by the card. Tasmania and Western Australia are the
only states that permit self-paced online completion; residents of other states must attend in real
time. If you live in Tasmania you qualify for the online course, and you will be asked to evidence your
Tasmanian residency at assessment.

*VerifiedSources: post-2019 construction-induction delivery policy; WorkSafe Tasmania. Verified 26 May 2026.*

## S3 · What you get, and how to get your card {#your-card}

**Answer capsule**
> Passing the course gives you a Statement of Attainment, not the physical card. To get the card you
> lodge that Statement in person at a Service Tasmania centre within 60 days, pay the $13.72 fee, and
> WorkSafe Tasmania issues your construction induction card. Miss the 60 days and you need a written
> declaration from the RTO.

**Body:**
This is the step most providers do not mention, and the one most likely to catch you out. In Tasmania
the card is not issued by the course provider. Blue Dog gives you the Statement of Attainment; the card
itself comes from WorkSafe Tasmania, through Service Tasmania, and only after you lodge.

The four steps: finish the course and pass the assessment; Blue Dog issues your Statement of Attainment;
within 60 days you take it in person to a Service Tasmania centre with your ID and the $13.72 fee; and
WorkSafe Tasmania processes it and posts your card.

You need original identity documents at lodgement, either two from the primary list or one primary plus
two secondary. The card does not expire, but it is voided if you leave the construction industry for two
consecutive years, after which the training must be redone.

**Note (caution):** The 60-day window runs from the date on your Statement of Attainment. Lodge after
it and Service Tasmania will also require a written declaration from Blue Dog confirming you completed
the training. Lodging in the first week is the simplest path.

*VerifiedSources: Service Tasmania — apply for a white card; WorkSafe Tasmania. Verified 22 July 2026 (fee), 27 May 2026 (process).*

## S5 · How does the course and assessment work? {#how-it-works}

**Answer capsule**
> You complete the course online, at your own pace, on any device. Assessment is a short video in which
> you demonstrate the safety steps, plus a set of online questions. Before you start you need a Unique
> Student Identifier and basic personal protective equipment to hand for the video.

**Body:**
The assessment is the part people wonder about with any online course, and it is also the reason
self-paced online is allowed at all. Since 2016 the White Card assessment has included a supervised
demonstration: you put on and correctly wear common site protective equipment on camera. That video,
with the online questions, is what makes an online White Card a real one rather than a box-ticking
exercise.

Have a hard hat, eye protection, hearing protection and a high-visibility top ready for the video, and
create your Unique Student Identifier before you begin, since nationally recognised training cannot be
issued without one. Blue Dog conducts and marks the assessment; ABE provides the platform and support.

*VerifiedSources: post-2019 construction-induction delivery policy (PPE demonstration requirement). Verified 26 May 2026.*

## S7 · White Card Tasmania FAQ {#faq}

Full answers in `src/data/faqs-white-card-tas.ts`. The first three are the ASQA-mandatory questions
(disclosure framework location 5); the last is the owner-builder cross-sell.

- **Who delivers this training?** → Blue Dog Training, RTO 31193, ASQA-accredited; delivers, assesses,
  issues the Statement of Attainment. ABE publishes/enrols and is not an RTO.
- **Who do I contact about a training or assessment problem?** → Blue Dog for training/assessment/SoA;
  ABE for enrolment/payment/access.
- **How do I verify the RTO?** → training.gov.au, RTO code 31193; listed current, CPCWHS1001 on scope.
- **How long does the course take?** → self-paced; work through it across one or two sittings. *(No
  fixed duration stated — completion time is an unconfirmed internal fact; see the flag below.)*
- **Can I do the online course if I do not live in Tasmania?** → the self-paced online course is for
  Tasmanian residents; other states have different delivery rules.
- **I am on a student visa. Can I enrol?** → yes; you need a USI and identity documents for lodgement.
- **When will my card arrive?** → after lodging at Service Tasmania, WorkSafe Tasmania posts it; if not
  within 60 days of lodgement, contact WorkSafe Tasmania.
- **I am also doing an owner builder permit. Do I need the White Card as well?** → yes; a TAS owner
  builder needs both. Cross-link to [Tasmanian owner builder course](/tas-owner-builder-course).
