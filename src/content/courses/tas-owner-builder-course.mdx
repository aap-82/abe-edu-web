<Section bg="bg-alt" id="regulator" marker="06" eyebrow="From CBOS" h2="What the regulator requires of you">
  <AnswerCapsule>When you hold an owner builder permit you take on the licensed builder’s responsibilities under Tasmanian law. You are responsible for site safety, for coordinating the licensed trades, and you stay liable to future owners for the building work for up to <b>ten years</b>.</AnswerCapsule>
  <div class="measure">
    <h3 class="h3">Your responsibilities as an owner builder</h3>
    <p>You carry the same duties as a licensed builder. Under the Work Health and Safety Act 2012 (Tas) you are the person conducting a business or undertaking (PCBU) for your site, so work health and safety is your responsibility. You coordinate the licensed trades, keep the build compliant with the National Construction Code, and you remain liable to any future owner for defective building work for up to ten years after the work is complete.</p>
    <h3 class="h3">The law behind the permit</h3>
    <p>Your permit is issued under the Occupational Licensing Act 2005 (Tas). Building work itself is regulated under the Building Act 2016 (Tas) and the Building Regulations 2016, which sort work into low-risk, notifiable and permit categories. If you enter a residential building contract over $20,000, the Residential Building Work Contracts and Dispute Resolution Act 2016 (Tas) sets minimum contract terms and a dispute-resolution scheme.</p>
