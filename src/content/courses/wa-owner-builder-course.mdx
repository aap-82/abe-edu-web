<Section id="need" marker="03" eyebrow="The threshold" h2="Do you need owner-builder approval, and this course?">
  <AnswerCapsule><b>You need approval if the building work is valued over $20,000</b> and you want to take on the builder’s role yourself rather than engage a registered building contractor. To get that approval you must show the required knowledge, which is where this course and a White Card come in. Then you apply to your local council for the building permit.</AnswerCapsule>
  <div class="measure">
    <p>Under the Building Act 2011, building work valued at more than <strong>$20,000</strong> must be carried out by a registered building contractor, unless you hold owner-builder approval for that work.</p>
    <p>Approval covers a detached house (Class 1a(i)), a Class 10 non-habitable structure such as a shed, garage, carport or patio, or a small commercial building of one or two storeys under 500 square metres.</p>
    <p>A Class 10a structure valued up to $50,000 does not require approval. You cannot split a project into smaller contracts to keep each piece under the threshold.</p>
    <p><b>Who can apply:</b> you must own, or be buying, the land; approval is granted to an individual, not a company or trust; you must intend to live in, occupy or use the building; and you can hold only one approval every six years.</p>
  </div>

<Section bg="bg-alt" id="responsibilities" marker="04" eyebrow="The role" h2="Your responsibilities as a WA owner-builder">
  <AnswerCapsule>As an owner-builder you become the person responsible for the build. That means holding the building permit and working to it, engaging licensed trades for electrical and plumbing work, meeting your work health and safety duties, supervising quality, carrying the right insurance, and fixing defects. These responsibilities are yours by law and cannot be handed off.</AnswerCapsule>
  <div class="measure">
    <p>Taking owner-builder approval means stepping into the builder’s shoes. You hold and comply with the building permit and its inspection stages.</p>
    <p>You engage appropriately licensed trades, and in WA electrical and plumbing work must be done by licensed practitioners.</p>
    <p>You carry the duties of a person conducting a business or undertaking under the Work Health and Safety Act 2020, keeping the site safe for anyone who works on or visits it.</p>
    <p>You supervise quality, keep records, arrange inspections and certifications, maintain insurance, and rectify defects.</p>
  </div>
  <Note variant="caution" label="Important"><b>Be accurate on your form:</b> provide false or misleading information in your application and you face penalties of up to $25,000 under the Building Services (Registration) Act 2011. The course walks through each duty so you take the role on with your eyes open.</Note>
