Blue Dog issues the Statement of Attainment. ABE Education publishes and supports the course and is not a registered training organisation.</AnswerCapsule>

<Section bg="bg-alt" id="accepted" marker="02" eyebrow="Accepted and online" h2="Is it accepted, and can I do it online in Tasmania?">
  <AnswerCapsule>Yes on both counts, with one condition. A White Card is nationally recognised the moment it is issued, accepted on every construction site in Australia. And because you live in Tasmania, you may complete the training fully online at your own pace. That online option is open to Tasmanian residents only.</AnswerCapsule>
  <div class="measure">
    <p>Most sites will not let you start work without a White Card, and the doubt people arrive with is whether an online one is treated any differently. It is not. Once issued, the card is the same credential however the training was delivered.</p>
  </div>

  <AnswerCapsule>Passing the course gives you a Statement of Attainment, not the physical card. To get the card you lodge that Statement in person at a Service Tasmania centre within 60 days, pay the $13.72 fee, and WorkSafe Tasmania issues your construction induction card. Miss the 60 days and you need a written declaration from the RTO.</AnswerCapsule>
  <div class="measure">
    <p>This is the step most providers do not mention, and the one most likely to catch you out. In Tasmania the card is not issued by the course provider. Blue Dog gives you the Statement of Attainment; the card itself comes from WorkSafe Tasmania, through Service Tasmania, and only after you lodge.</p>
    <p>You need original identity documents at lodgement, either two from the primary list or one primary plus two secondary. The card does not expire, but it is voided if you leave the construction industry for two consecutive years, after which the training must be redone.</p>
  </div>

<Section id="how-it-works" marker="05" eyebrow="How it works" h2="How does the course and assessment work?">
  <AnswerCapsule>You complete the course online, at your own pace, on any device. Assessment is a short video in which you demonstrate the safety steps, plus a set of online questions. Before you start you need a Unique Student Identifier and basic personal protective equipment to hand for the video.</AnswerCapsule>
  <div class="measure">
    <p>The assessment is the part people wonder about with any online course, and it is also the reason self-paced online is allowed at all. Since 2016 the White Card assessment has included a supervised demonstration: you put on and correctly wear common site protective equipment on camera. That video, with the online questions, is what makes an online White Card a real one rather than a box-ticking exercise.</p>
    <p>Have a hard hat, eye protection, hearing protection and a high-visibility top ready for the video, and create your Unique Student Identifier before you begin, since nationally recognised training cannot be issued without one. Blue Dog conducts and marks the assessment; ABE Education provides the platform and support.</p>
  </div>
