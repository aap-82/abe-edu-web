<Section id="what-counts" bg="bg-warm" marker="02" total="06" eyebrow="What counts" h2="Do all twelve points actually count?">
  <AnswerCapsule>Yes. CBOS caps some ways of earning points, not some subjects: attending WorkSafe Tasmania events is capped at six a year, trade journals at three. Approved online courses carry no annual cap, and health and safety is a recognised CPD category, so all twelve of these count.</AnswerCapsule>

  <p>The distinction matters when you are topping up a short year. CBOS limits what you can claim for the things practitioners do incidentally: six points a year for WorkSafe Tasmania sessions, four for research, three for reading trade journals, two for association membership. Those limits attach to the activity, not to the topic.</p>

  <p>Course points are not limited that way. An endorsed online course earns a point per hour whatever its subject, so a bundle weighted toward safety counts exactly the same as one weighted toward technical content. What does matter is that each course is approved in its own right, which is why the table above carries a date for every one.</p>

  <Note variant="note" label="Where the caps do bite">If you were planning to make up a shortfall by reading journals or attending WorkSafe Month, those routes run out quickly: three and six points a year respectively. Course points do not.</Note>

  <VerifiedSources date="23 July 2026" facts="which CPD activities carry annual caps, and that endorsed online courses do not" sources={cbosSource} />
</Section>
