<Section id="your-year" marker="01" total="05" eyebrow="Your obligation" h2="Does twelve points cover your whole year?">
  <AnswerCapsule>Twelve points meets a builder's requirement in full. If you also hold a building designer or architect licence you need 20, not 32: CBOS counts the highest single requirement you hold, never the sum. A building surveyor or engineer needs 30, so this bundle covers part of that year.</AnswerCapsule>

  <FactGrid items={frontmatter.requirementRows} />

  <p>The rule on multiple licences is the one most often got wrong, and it works in your favour. A person licensed as both a builder and a building designer owes 20 points for the year, not the 32 they would owe if the requirements stacked. Some of that learning should still relate to each type of work you are licensed for.</p>

  <p>If you hold only a builder licence, twelve points is your year, and this bundle is built to be exactly that and no more. If you hold a surveyor or engineer licence at 30 points, buy it knowing it covers twelve of them.</p>

  <VerifiedSources date="12 July 2026" facts="the points required for each licence category, and the multi-licence rule" sources={cbosSource} />
</Section>

<Section id="what-counts" bg="bg-warm" marker="02" total="05" eyebrow="The category caps" h2="How many of these points will actually count?">
  <AnswerCapsule>CBOS counts at most four work health and safety points in a renewal year, whatever you complete beyond that. So the mix in a bundle matters as much as the total, because the points you are audited on are the ones that fall inside the caps, not the ones on your receipt.</AnswerCapsule>

  <p>This is the part most bundles do not mention. A twelve-point bundle made mostly of safety courses does not give a practitioner twelve countable points, because the WHS category is capped at four a year. The total on the invoice and the total CBOS accepts are different numbers.</p>

  <p>ABE mixes safety with trade and technical topics for this reason, rather than filling a bundle with whichever courses were cheapest to build.</p>

  <Note variant="caution" label="Before you buy">If your renewal is close, check what you have already recorded this year. If you have used your safety points elsewhere, the useful part of any bundle is the technical content, and that is worth knowing before you pay rather than after.</Note>

  <VerifiedSources date="12 July 2026" facts="the four-point annual cap on work health and safety CPD" sources={cbosSource} />
</Section>

<Section id="how" bg="bg-alt" marker="04" total="05" eyebrow="How it works" h2="How does the bundle work?">
  <AnswerCapsule>Enrol once, work through the twelve courses in any order at your own pace, complete the assessment on each, and download a certificate as you finish each one. Keep those certificates with your own CPD record, because in Tasmania the record is yours to hold.</AnswerCapsule>

  <p>About ten hours covers all twelve, measured from what students actually spend on the platform rather than from a nominal course length. Most people do not sit down and complete a bundle in one stretch, and they do not need to: the courses are independent, so you can finish one in a sitting and come back to the next.</p>

  <Stepper steps={frontmatter.howItWorksSteps} />

  <p>Recording CPD sits with the licence holder, not the provider and not the employer. If you are employed, keep your own copies and make sure your employer's register reflects them. If you are a sole practitioner, the whole record is yours. Certificates are portable between employers, which matters if you change jobs mid-cycle.</p>
