faqs:
  - q: "Which state's course do I need?"
    a: "The one for the state where the building work is, not where you live. If you live in Victoria and are building in Tasmania, you need the Tasmanian course and a Tasmanian permit."
    open: true
  - q: "Can I use one state's certificate in another state?"
    a: "No. Each regulator approves its own course against its own legislation, and a certificate is only recognised by the regulator it was designed for. If you build in two states you need both courses."
  - q: "How long is my certificate valid?"
    a: "It varies by state, and it is measured back from the date you apply rather than forward from the date you pass. Queensland and the ACT accept a certificate up to five years old. Tasmania asks that a home owner builder complete the course within the twelve months before lodging. Western Australia issues yours the same day for your Form 75."
  - q: "Do I complete the course before or after I apply?"
    a: "Before. Every one of these regulators wants the certificate lodged as part of the application, so the course is a prerequisite, not a follow-up."
  - q: "Is ABE a registered training organisation?"
    a: "No, and these courses are not nationally recognised training. They are the regulator-approved or regulator-accepted owner builder courses for their states, issued as a Certificate of Completion. That is exactly what each of these applications asks for."
