faqs:
  # Question-shaped to match how the demand is actually phrased. Both duration queries sit
  # at strong positions already, so this protects them rather than chasing something new.
  - q: "How long does the owner builder course take?"
    a: "Most people finish in one sitting. The Queensland, Western Australian and Tasmanian courses are built around four hours of content, and the ACT course around three. Every one of them is online and self-paced, so you can stop whenever you like and pick up on any device with your progress saved. There is no classroom attendance and no scheduled start date."
  - q: "Which state's course do I need?"
    a: "The one for the state where the building work is, not where you live. If you live in Victoria and are building in Tasmania, you need the Tasmanian course and a Tasmanian permit."
    open: true
  - q: "Can I use one state's certificate in another state?"
    a: "No. Each regulator approves its own course against its own legislation, and a certificate is only recognised by the regulator it was designed for. If you build in two states you need both courses."
  - q: "How long is my certificate valid?"
    a: "It varies by state, and it is measured back from the date you apply rather than forward from the date you pass. Queensland and the ACT accept a certificate up to five years old. Tasmania asks that a home owner builder complete the course within the twelve months before lodging. Western Australia's Building Services Board accepts a WA-specific course completed within the last two years, alongside a White Card. Complete the course before you lodge, and check the window still covers you if the application has been sitting a while."
  - q: "Do I complete the course before or after I apply?"
    a: "Before. Every one of these regulators wants the certificate lodged as part of the application, so the course is a prerequisite, not a follow-up."
  - q: "Is ABE a registered training organisation?"
    a: "No, and these courses are not nationally recognised training. They are the regulator-approved or regulator-accepted owner builder courses for their states, issued as a Certificate of Completion. That is exactly what each of these applications asks for."
